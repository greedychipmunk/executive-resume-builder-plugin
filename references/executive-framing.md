# Executive Framing Reference

Senior and C-suite resumes are judged on a different axis than individual-contributor
resumes. The reader (a board member, CEO, investor, or executive recruiter) is
scanning for **scope, business impact, and leadership judgment** — not task lists.
This reference covers how to frame an executive narrative.

## The shift: from *doing* to *directing*

| IC framing | Executive framing |
|------------|-------------------|
| What you personally built | What your organization achieved under your leadership |
| Technical/functional tasks | Business outcomes (revenue, margin, growth, risk) |
| Tools and technologies | Strategy, vision, and operating decisions |
| "I delivered X" | "I built the team/system that delivered X at scale" |

Executives are credited for **leverage**: the results produced *through* people,
capital, and strategy. Bullets should foreground scope and outcome.

## What executive readers look for

- **Scale of responsibility** — org size, budget/P&L owned, revenue influenced,
  geographic/market footprint, breadth of functions reported to you.
- **Business impact** — revenue growth, profitability, cost structure, valuation,
  market share, retention — tied to the company's strategic goals.
- **Transformation** — turnarounds, scaling (e.g. "Series A to IPO", "$10M→$100M"),
  reorganizations, M&A integration, market entry, cultural change.
- **Leadership** — teams built, executives hired/developed, board/investor
  relationships, cross-functional and external influence.
- **Strategic judgment** — bets made, trade-offs navigated, what you chose *not* to
  do, how you set direction under uncertainty.

## Structure for an executive resume

1. **Headline** — title + domain + the one-line value proposition.
   *e.g. "Chief Operating Officer — Scaled two SaaS companies from $20M to $200M ARR."*
2. **Executive summary** — 3–5 lines: who you are, the scale you operate at, your
   signature impact, and your leadership style/focus. No objective statements.
3. **Core competencies** — a compact scannable line/cluster of leadership and domain
   strengths (P&L Management, GTM Strategy, Org Design, M&A, Board Relations).
4. **Experience** — reverse-chronological. For each role, open with a one-line
   **context/mandate** (company stage, size, what you were brought in to do), then
   3–6 outcome bullets ordered by impact.
5. **Board/Advisory, Education, Recognition** — concise, lower in the document.

## The role-context line

Executives often join at an inflection point; state the mandate so outcomes have
meaning:

> **Chief Revenue Officer — Northwind (Series C SaaS, $40M ARR, 600 FTE)**
> Recruited by the board to rebuild GTM after two flat years.
> - Grew net-new ARR 62% to $65M in 7 quarters by rebuilding sales, RevOps, and pricing.
> - Hired and developed a 9-person leadership team; cut rep ramp time 40%.

## Quantifying at the executive level

Pull the biggest, business-relevant numbers (see `quantification-guide.md`):
revenue/ARR, growth %, P&L size, headcount, market expansion, fundraising,
valuation, retention/NRR, margin. Tie them to **strategic outcomes**, not activity.

## Language calibration

- **Confident, not boastful.** Let the numbers carry the weight; avoid adjectives
  like "world-class", "visionary", "rockstar".
- **Strategic verbs:** spearheaded, scaled, transformed, restructured, championed,
  pioneered, instituted, repositioned (see `action-verbs.md`).
- **Own results honestly.** Distinguish what you *owned* vs. *influenced*; boards
  check references. Credibility is the executive's core currency.
- **Cut the weeds.** Remove hands-on tool lists and granular tasks unless they're
  strategically relevant; they shrink perceived altitude.

## Common executive-resume mistakes

- Reads like a senior-IC resume (tasks, tools, no business outcomes).
- Buries scope — no org size, budget, or revenue context anywhere.
- Generic summary full of buzzwords instead of a specific value proposition.
- Too long or too dense; an executive resume should still be skimmable in 30 seconds.
- Inflated/sole-credit claims that won't survive a reference check.
